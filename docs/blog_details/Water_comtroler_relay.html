My First ESP8266 Project: Controlling an Electric Water Pump via Telegram

(Technically not my first project but this the first one I am loading about)

So I had a problem with managing the water for my whole house (we use a lot of water)

so we had to turn the pump on and off often and it was hard to manage with all the other things we had to do

The Problem

  • Manually turning on and off the water pump was inconvenient.
  • countinuous run for hours without know how turen it on or of, why and when(we are human erroes are commen)
  • and sometimes we forget to turn it off, wasting water and energy adn over fulling the tank multiple times
  • the tank is in 8 floor??? (idk its complicated) so it not possible to check the tank level manually

The Needs for the solution

  • Automate the water pump control
  • keep a log when it turned off and on
  • multiple people need to have access from anywhere in the house or outside(or world lol)

What i did to create solution

  • already had an ESP8266 module(because cheap)
  • used an relay module to control the pump
  • and using Telegram bot api(because free)

The Components

  • ESP8266 Module (node mcu)
  • Relay Module x 2 (12volt controlled and 5 volt controlled)
  • Electric Water Pump(already there)
  • voltage booster module (to step up the 5V to 12V)
  • voltage regulator (to step down the 5V to 3.3V for the esp8266)
  • Power Supply(cheap 5 volt micro usb adapter)
  • Telegram Bot API(free!!!! with limites ofc ther is no such things as free lunch)
    • (I am not including the cost because i dont rember but you can check out my code at my github)

The first Prototype

using a bread board and a esp8266 i made a simple prototype to control the water pump

The Final Version

So after a lot of soldering, took me a day to make the final version
I made a handmade pcb and soldered all the wires myself
I also made a simple enclosure to protect the electronics from dust and water(A green plastic box , i dont have 3d printer)
To be honest the orginal plan was to make an the 12 volt relay controled by an mosfet
but the shop didn't have the parts I needed so I had to improvise(there is nothing bad about improvising)
I used a 5 volt relay instead which controles the 12 volt relay
