Why I Broke My First ESP8266 (And What I Learned)

Well it all started with me trying to power an LED array like the long PCB with LED (which I got from taking apart an old led light,broken).

So i was trying to controle the led with my esp8266 with an GPIO pin(my first mistake), and it did light up but was dim, so i thought

I should power it with a 5V source directly to the Vin pin of the esp8266, and yes it worked for a few seconds and a capacitor on the esp started smoking

and yep (my second mistake) I saw it burn and the esp8266 was dead.

What I Learned

  1. Always check the voltage and current requirements of your components before connecting them.
  2. Use a separate power supply for high-power components like LED arrays instead of powering them directly from the microcontroller.
  3. Consider using a transistor or a MOSFET to switch high-power loads instead of driving them directly from GPIO pins.
  4. Double-check your wiring and connections to avoid accidental shorts or incorrect connections.

In conclusion, breaking my first ESP8266 was a valuable learning experience that taught me important lessons about electronics and circuit design. By being more cautious and informed in my future projects, I can avoid similar mistakes and build more reliable and efficient systems.
